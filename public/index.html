Who We Are
FILE REF:KAAAND-00-MANIFESTO
SUBJECT:India's underground culture magazine
ORIGIN:
CLEARANCE:PUBLIC RELEASE AUTHORISED

KAAAND exists because the culture that is actually happening in this country — on the floors, in the back rooms, in the Dharavi workspaces, in the Delhi basement parties that end at 1 AM because the city has a curfew designed to kill the scene — has been systematically invisible in every publication that claims to cover it.

The mainstream media covers Indian youth culture from the outside in. We don't. We are not a trend report. We are not a discovery platform for brands looking to seem relevant. We are a document, assembled from the inside, by people who were there.

We cover fashion because in India, how you dress is a political statement whether you intend it to be or not — and the most interesting people are the ones who intend it. We cover music because the underground electronic scene was built by people who were told this country wasn't ready for it, and they built it anyway.

We are not neutral. KAAAND is a record of what this country makes for itself, by itself, on its own terms. The kaaand has started. It doesn't stop.

Articles — Issue 00

The Thrift Economy

Arjun Mehta - Fashion

Sarojini Nagar on a Saturday has always been about knowing something the next person doesn't. What changed is who's in on it — and what they're doing with what they learn. The thrift economy didn't come from sustainability discourse or conscious consumption rhetoric. It came from people who were broke, had sharp eyes, and refused to look cheap.

Floors Without Walls

Priya Nair - Rave Culture

Goa trance was made in India by people who weren't Indian, on dancefloors the locals weren't allowed onto, from spiritual imagery taken without asking. The genre went global. India got left outside the story it generated. The scene that exists today decided not to wait for credit.

No Logo Required

Kabir Singh - Brands

The homegrown Indian fashion label has a specific set of associations now. The founding story. The Instagram drop. The influencer seed list. The collab reveal. Most of it is performance. And then there are the labels that simply make the thing and let it speak — the ones you find out about because someone whose taste you trust is wearing something you cannot place.

The Psy-Trance Revival

Ananya Rao - Music

Psytrance was made in India by people who were not Indian, packaged for a global market, and then abandoned when the market moved on. The genre went everywhere. India stayed outside its own origin story. A new generation of Indian producers has decided that's over.

Dressing Against the Grain

Siya Kapoor - Identity

India decriminalised homosexuality in 2018. Not 1918. 2018. Eight years ago. The queer communities in Delhi, Mumbai, Bengaluru, and Kolkata who built their visual identities while that law was still on the books did not wait for permission to dress. What they built — under conditions that required creativity and courage simultaneously — is what the rest of Indian fashion is now borrowing without saying so.
